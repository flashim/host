CPE Toolkit

Introducing a new, streamlined CPE process

The training session ends – and CPE Certificates of Completion are sent directly to your client – quickly and efficiently!

Our clients have asked for it, and some of our competitors have similar programs in place.

Sound unrealistic? Not anymore. In fact, clients who opt to access the web-based self study training courses on KPMG Learning can receive their CPE Certificates of Completion immediately upon successful completion of the self study!

A key feature on the recently launched here as part of the KPMG Learning internal portal, the KPMG Learning CPE Toolkit contains specific guidance on the steps necessary before, during and after delivering training to make that your clients receive their CPE Certificates in record time.

The new Toolkit specifically focuses on enhancing some key areas that have historically slowed CPE processing times:

  1. Submission Challenges: NASBA guidelines require that KPMG document certain details about each program where CPE is granted. The Toolkit now helps make that documentation process easier – especially when engagement teams leverage courses from the KPMG Learning catalog. Ultimate responsibility for accurate and timely submission remains with the engagement team.

  2. Processing Challenges: In 2011 more than 100,000 CPE hours were awarded to clients and targets. The HRSC processing team has met challenges in keeping up with ever-increasing demand for client CPE processing. In 2012, additional resources dedicated to processing client CPE have been added.

Regardless of whether you deliver the training to your clients or if they access it directly on KPMG Learning, their Certificates of Completion will be executed more rapidly than has been possible in the past.

Of course, the KPMG Learning Management Team is available to help you quickly address any CPE-related questions.

Visit CPE Toolkit for more information and the specific steps involved in the KPMG Learning CPE qualification process.

KPMG is an accredited organization of the National Association of State Boards of Accountancy (NASBA) and can assess and award CPE credit for any KPMG designed and delivered training. Under our agreement with NASBA, KPMG may grant CPE credit only for programs that meet certain requirements related to content development, instructional design, and the delivery of course materials.


KPMG is registered with the National Association of State Boards of Accountancy (NASBA), as a Quality Assurance Service (QAS) sponsor of continuing professional education. State boards of accountancy have final authority on the acceptance of individual courses for CPE credit. Complaints regarding QAS program sponsors may be submitted to NASBA through its website: www.learningmarket.org
