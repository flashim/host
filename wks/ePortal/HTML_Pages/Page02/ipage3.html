Prepare for your session

There are many issues to consider when preparing to deliver a training event to a client.

While in many cases the client will manage the majority of logistics related to the training, KPMG is responsible for ensuring that the course gets delivered smoothly and effectively to the learners. The first step is to make sure your participants are fully prepared for the session, that your venue is sufficient, and that your instructors (even if you are the instructor!) are prepared to teach.

The rollout of a training program often becomes a complex, choreographed activity. Below are some tips to consider pre-delivery. Be sure to involve your client and obtain the necessary approval prior to making final decisions.

Pre-Delivery Tips Done Completed Date Comments
Establish the timetable for the course.
  • Start and end times;
  • Breakfast, lunch and breaks (morning and afternoon as appropriate)
    
Schedule the courses and reserve on-site (client site) or off-site classrooms (hotels, conference centers).    
Notify learners and their supervisors about the course. If CPE will be provided, the KPMG Learning Course Announcement template must be used. See the CPE Toolkit tab for all CPE requirements.    
Select instructors. All instructors should review the material prior to the session. Strong instructor preparation is critical to the program success. Review the CPE Toolkit tab for further information on documentation requirements regarding instructor qualifications.    
Determine whether class materials will be printed or provided to participants electronically.    
Ensure site will have internet-ready computers; proxima and screen; flipcharts and markers.    
Manage travel and expenses for the trainers and/or learners.    
Order food for breakfast, lunch, breaks as determined during planning.    
If revisions are made (>15% change) to existing KPMG Learning courses, obtain a QA review. See QA review tab for details.    
Develop and print all training session materials (including leader guides, participant material, and handouts).    
Arrange for participant name tags and tent cards (as appropriate).    
Copy important program related documents onto jump drive for easy access while on-site.    
Ensure facilities are adequate for the training. Factors to consider include:
  • Sufficient space for all attendees to sit comfortably during instruction.
  • Sufficient room set-up for participants to interact with one another.
  • Enough equipment for all attendees and demonstration equipment for the instructor/facilitator (if applicable).
  • Space and facilities for small group exercises or hands-on training using equipment as part of activity-based learning.
  • Equipment, technical support, and resources sufficient to support training via technology, such as during instructor presentations or web-based training used by students to enhance learning (if applicable).
    
Arrange for all CPE required documents (i.e., Participant Sign-in Sheet, course evaluations)
