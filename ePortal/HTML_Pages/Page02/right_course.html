Determine the Right Course

With your analysis completed and a good understanding of your client’s particular need, below are questions that you might ask yourself to determine which (if any) of the KPMG Learning courses is the right course for your client.

Question Considerations
  • How should ideas be presented to learners?
  • What level of skill/ knowledge is most appropriate for your audience?
 KPMG Learning courses have been organized so that the ideas are presented in a logical fashion and the material is at the right level of depth for the audience.

KPMG Learning courses on a particular topic are grouped by knowledge level – overview, basic, intermediate, advanced, and update. This information helps make choosing a course that best reflects the audience identified in your analysis easier than starting from scratch.
  • Will Continuing Professional Education (CPE) credit be awarded?
 The majority of KPMG Learning training courses are CPE-worthy. Each KPMG Learning course has its estimated number of CPE listed in the course abstract. So if your client is only interested in courses that are CPE-worthy, use the CPE filter function within the Search feature to find courses that meet your client's CPE needs.

Note: There are several requirements that must be met before CPE credit can be awarded to clients for successful completion of KPMG-delivered training. Review the CPE tab for more information and specific requirements that must be completed prior to, during and after delivering training sessions to clients.
  • What training format will work best for my client?
  • What mix of courses will be helpful?
Various delivery formats of the same topic may exist within the KPMG Learning catalog. So when you search on a topic using the Search feature, be sure to review the different delivery formats to find the one that best fits your client's needs.

Delivery modes include:
  • Instructor-led (conducted by engagement team)
  • Instructor-led – public (i.e., Executive Education)
  • Webcast (i.e., KPMG Institutes)
  • Web-based self study
  • Podcasts
In some cases, a combination of two modes might be the best solution for your client. For example, participating in a brief webcast or self-study could be followed up by a half-day Instructor-led session that would allow your clients to further explore a particular topic and discuss with you how the issues presented might directly impact their organization.

Important!KPMG Learning is a new service of the firm. While the catalog is continually growing, it may not currently contain a training course that will meet the needs of your client. If this is the case and you would like assistance in developing a course on a topic KPMG Learning does not yet cover, don't hesitate to contact the KPMG Learning Management Team.

Once you have selected the right course (or courses) for your client, use the following guidance to determine your immediate next steps.

Format Icon Format Next Steps
KPMG Institutes Webcasts

Webcasts are offered on a specific date and are available via a link from KPMG Learning to the KPMG Institutes home page.

Consider sharing the course abstract with your client or directing them to the KPMG Institutes home page where they can register for and attend a particular Webcast.

Your client or target will need to become a member of the KPMG Institutes to participate in any Webcasts.

Note: The KPMG Learning catalog currently carries primarily technical accounting courses, therefore not every Webcast offered by KPMG Institutes is offered through KPMG Learning. KPMG Institutes carries a complete listing of all Webcasts that might be of interest to you client.
KPMG Podcasts

Podcasts are brief audio snippets that provide quick highlights or overviews on current and relevant topics of interest to business professionals. Podcasts are free of charge.

Note: Podcasts are not CPE eligible. Consider sharing the course abstract with your client or direct them to the KPMG Learning external Website where they can access the podcast directly.
On Demand Web-based Training (WBT)

Visitors to the external KPMG Learning Website can launch and complete WBT courses directly from the Web site. The internal portal contains abstracts that you can share with your client. In addition, if you would like to preview a WBT prior to recommending it to your client, you can do so from the internal portal.

WBTs vary in pricing. Retail pricing is available via the KPMG Learning external website. Bulk and special pricing is available in certain instances.

CPE Certificates of Completion are available immediately when a client successfully completes a WBT.
Executive Education

Executive Education (now under the KPMG Learning umbrella of training services) offers Group Live, Instructor-Led CPE Credit accounting courses, seminars, workshops and update conferences for corporate accountants and financial executives.

Consider sharing the course abstract with your client, providing the current Executive Education brochure, or directing them to the Executive Education website.
Downloadable course materials If one of the courses you identify is "classroom ready" – meaning that it is ready for engagement team delivery, save the course to your "My Interests List." From this list, you can simply download the course materials to your computer. Then follow the Steps to Delivering Quality Training to make sure you are well-equipped to deliver the high-quality training our clients have come to expect from KPMG.
