Risk & Independence

KPMG's ability to deliver insightful, accurate and complete knowledge content to the market in a timely manner is a key performance indicator for the firm. The training courses featured in the KPMG Learning catalog are one method of delivering this knowledge content to our clients and targets. But knowing how to obtain the appropriate internal approvals prior to providing training externally is vital to our organization.

All courses in the KPMG Learning catalog have been reviewed for currency and technical accuracy, and are approved for use with both clients and targets. No additional content approvals are required if the courses are delivered as-designed.

In some cases, as-designed training content may not meet the specific needs of your client or target, and tailoring or revision will be necessary.

According to firm policy, any significant revisions should be reviewed by the engagement partner and if the partner deems necessary, by an additional field subject matter professional with considerable knowledge and experience in the specific content area.

In addition, revising a course to the extent that the field of study is impacted or the length of the program increases or decreases more than 15% may have Continuing Professional Education (CPE) implications (refer to the CPE Toolkit section for more information).

One of the most important aspects of our "promise of professionalism" is the requirement to be independent in fact and perception. Compliance with independence standards is a professional responsibility prerequisite to providing audit, review and other attestation services. This pledge to independence is essential to serving the best interests of our clients and their investors -- and to furthering KPMGâ€™s reputation for integrity and quality.

In certain instances, delivery of training to our audit clients (public and non-public) may raise independence questions. Please refer to the Independence Matrix to determine whether your particular client situation is subject to Audit Committee pre-approval, Sentinel, or CLEAS.
